Practice

An advisory practice for the years before, during, and after a defining transaction.

Our practice is organized around the questions that surround a generational liquidity event — the planning that precedes it, the structures that survive it, and the legacy that follows.

01

Pre-Transaction Strategy

Strategic analysis for principals whose facts do not fit a standard template. Most of our work in this area is undertaken in coordination with the client's existing CPA or tax counsel, and is intended to address questions that sit at the edge of those relationships.

Our role is to bring sustained, focused attention to a small number of questions — and to do so on a timeline that allows the answers to actually shape the transaction.

  • Pre-transaction structural review of closely-held businesses
  • Entity organization and rationalization analysis
  • Qualified small business stock considerations
  • Sale structure analysis (installment, deferred, contingent consideration)
  • State residency considerations in advance of a transaction
  • Charitable strategy as it intersects with a transaction timeline
  • Coordination with existing CPAs, transactional counsel, and wealth advisors
02

Estate & Legacy Planning

Generational planning for families whose principal asset is a closely-held interest, a recently-monetized concentrated position, or an operating enterprise that is intended to outlive its founder. The technical questions are familiar; the difficult questions are governance.

We approach this work as a long, patient conversation, not a transaction. The structures matter, but the structures only function if the family understands them and the next generation is prepared to operate within them.

  • Generational transfer strategy around a concentrated holding
  • Trust structure analysis, including directed-trustee considerations
  • Family governance frameworks and intergenerational education
  • Charitable lead and remainder strategy; private foundation considerations
  • Succession analysis for operating businesses passing within a family
  • Coordination with corporate trustees, wealth advisors, and family offices
03

Exit Planning

Pre-transaction analysis for founders, families, and majority owners preparing for a sale, recapitalization, or succession of a closely-held enterprise. Engagements in this area typically begin twelve to thirty-six months before a contemplated transaction and run alongside the principal's bankers, transactional counsel, and CPAs.

The work is not deal advisory. The work is the planning that surrounds the deal — the questions a banker is not retained to answer and the questions transactional counsel cannot reach in the timeline of a live process.

  • Pre-LOI tax and estate posture review
  • Sale-process readiness for the principal, separate from the company
  • Liquidity event analysis for the family balance sheet
  • Coordination with investment bankers, M&A counsel, and wealth advisors
  • Post-closing entity rationalization analysis
  • Long-form document review for the principal's personal positions
