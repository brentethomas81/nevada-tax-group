The Firm

A specialist bench, organized around a defined set of complex questions.

Nevada Tax Group is a private advisory practice built for the planning that surrounds a single, generational transaction. Our work is technical, our engagements are intentional, and our relationships are long.

Philosophy

The work that compounds happens before the closing.

Most of the families and operators who become clients of the firm have already engaged sophisticated counsel and accountants. They are not looking for a primary advisor. They are looking for a second bench — a specialist group whose only job is to think, in detail, about the planning decisions that will outlive the transaction itself.

Our role is to do that planning work early, in coordination with a principal's existing advisors, and with the care a defining transaction warrants.

How We Work

By referral, by design.

The firm does not solicit business directly and does not maintain a marketing function. New engagements arrive through three channels: referrals from professional firms with whom we have an existing working relationship; introductions from prior clients; and inbound inquiries from advisors who have a specific client situation in mind.

We decline more engagements than we accept. The reasons are straightforward: the work is technical and time-intensive, and we hold our active matters at any given time to an intentional limit. We are also disciplined about fit — there are situations where another firm is genuinely a better choice, and we say so.

Each engagement we accept is governed by a written engagement letter that defines the scope and basis of the work. The firm's compensation is set at the outset and is in no part contingent on the outcome of any transaction or the implementation of any product.

Independence

We sell counsel, not products.

The firm does not maintain affiliations with broker-dealers, insurance companies, registered investment advisors, or any organization whose compensation depends on a client implementing a particular financial product. Our engagement letters are explicit on this point, and we are happy to address the question directly with prospective clients or their advisors.

When a particular planning approach involves a product — a trust, an entity, an insurance contract, a charitable vehicle — we identify the providers our clients have available, document the considerations on either side, and let the client and their primary advisors make the implementation decision with full information. The firm does not receive compensation in connection with that selection.

Engagement

We accept new engagements only by introduction.

If you are a professional advisor with a client situation, or a principal who has been referred to the firm, we welcome a brief, confidential conversation.
